AOS-referenced timing guide for green pod culture and flasking decisions.

‚ö° Fast (2-6 months)
  • Disa: 2-8 weeks
  • Ludisia: 30-40 days (Jewel orchids)
  • Phragmipedium: 3-5 months
  • Paphiopedilum micranthum: ~4 months
  • Sophronitis: 5-6 months
‚è±Ô∏è Medium (6-9 months)
  • Phalaenopsis: 6-8 months
    Green pod optimal: 6-7 months
  • Vanda: 6-8 months
    Green pod optimal: 6-7 months
  • Oncidium: 6-8 months (variable)
  • Dendrobium: 6-9 months (species dependent)
üêå Slow (9-18 months)
  • Cattleya (Bifoliate): 4-5 months green pod
    vs 9+ months full maturity
  • Cattleya (Standard): 9-13 months
    Green pod: 4-5 months
  • Cymbidium: 9-12 months
  • Paphiopedilum complex: 5-18 months
    P. lowii: ~5 months, P. tigrinum: 14 months
üå± Green Pod Harvest Indicators
  • Capsule swollen with color change from dark green
  • Three ribs joining capsule segments begin to lift
  • Seeds change from white to cream/yellow
  • Before any splitting or yellowing at pod ends
  • Capsule walls thin and translucent when backlit

Critical Rule: Female parent determines maturation timing in intergeneric crosses

‚ö° Green Pod Advantages
  • Harvest 6-8 months earlier than natural dehiscence
  • Higher germination rates than dry seed methods
  • Eliminates seed sterilization needs
  • Faster mother plant recovery
  • Dramatically reduced flowering time for seedlings

Note: Single opportunity - no storage option, requires immediate processing
